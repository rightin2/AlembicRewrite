What it does

A rewriting layer over every app you use

One tool, many styles. Each style is yours to shape: its own prompt, its own model, its own hotkey.

Silent, instant rewrite

Press Cmd+Shift+R and your selected text is replaced in place, no window, no confirmation. Undo from the menu bar within your chosen window, and your clipboard is always restored afterwards.

Style palette

Press Cmd+Shift+E for a searchable palette of all your styles. Type to filter, arrow to choose, press Return to run.

Review and iterate

For styles you set to review first, a panel shows the original beside the rewrite. Not right yet? Iterate with a follow-up instruction, then accept.

Unlimited custom styles

Build as many styles as you like. Give each one its own prompt template, pick Claude or OpenAI and the exact model, set the temperature, and bind a direct hotkey.

The AlembicRewriter style

The flagship default style turns a rough prompt into an effective one using the Directional Prompting method, so you can draft loosely and let the style sharpen it.

Local-first, bring your own key

Your API keys and history live only on your Mac, with history retention you control. Requests go straight from your machine to Anthropic or OpenAI, with no vendor server in the middle. A running cost meter tracks your spend, and a monthly cap stops rewrites at the ceiling you set.

Get started

Installing takes about a minute

Download the disk image, drag the app across, and add your own API key. Nothing to sign up for.

  1. Download the DMG

    Grab AlembicRewrite.dmg (6.9 MB) from the download button above, then open it and drag AlembicRewrite into your Applications folder.

  2. Approve the first launch in Privacy & Security

    Because the app is not notarised through the App Store, macOS blocks it once. Double-click the app and let macOS refuse it, then open System Settings > Privacy & Security, scroll down, and click Open Anyway. On macOS 14 you can instead right-click the app and choose Open twice. Once only, either way.

  3. Grant Accessibility permission

    On first run the app asks for Accessibility access, which is how it reads your selection and pastes the rewrite back. Approve it in System Settings when prompted.

  4. Add your own API key

    Open Settings from the menu-bar icon and paste an Anthropic or OpenAI key into the API Keys tab. The key is stored locally and used only for your own direct requests.

  5. Select some text and press the hotkey

    Highlight a sentence anywhere, press Cmd+Shift+R for an instant rewrite, or Cmd+Shift+E to pick a style. That is the whole loop.

Bring your own key

AlembicRewrite has no accounts and no subscription. It talks directly to Anthropic or OpenAI using a key you supply, so you pay those providers for exactly what you use, at cost.

Your keys, your rewrite history, and your styles are held only on your Mac. No client text is routed through any server the app's author controls.

Need a key? Create one in the Anthropic Console or the OpenAI platform dashboard, then paste it into Settings.
